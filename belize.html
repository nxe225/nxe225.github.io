Belize Things To Know

Section 1 - Belize Economy

Agriculture

Agriculture is a leading force of the economy and allowed Belize to have a strong post-pandemic rebound. Innovative and ecological bond restructuring significantly lowered public debt and expanded marine protections.

Tourism

Tourism has become the mainstay of the economy. Popular services include diving at the Belize Barrier Reef, exploring Mayan ruins, and eco-friendly activities like wildlife tours and cave tubing. Belize also attracts visitors with luxury resorts, cruise ship excursions, and growing interest in agritourism and wellness retreats.

GPD Growth

The real GPD growth rate increased 17.1% in 2021, 8.73% in 2022, and 4.54% in 2023. An increase in GDP is beneficial because it often leads to more jobs, higher incomes, and improved living standards.



Section 2 - Belize Culture

Languages

Over 60% of the population in Belize is speaking english, which is the countries native language. However, Kriol is widely spoken as the everyday language for most Belizeans. Additionally, Spanish is commonly spoken, especially in the northern and western regions.

Ethnic Groups

Belize is made up of 52.9% Mestizo, 25.9% Creole, 11.3%, 6.1% Garifuna, 3.9% East Indian, 3.6% Mennonite, 1.2%, Asian 1%, 1.2% white, and 0.3% is unknown.

Migration

Migration continues to transform Belize's population. About 16% of Belizeans live abroad, while immigrants constitute approximately 15% of Belize's population. Migration significantly affects Belize by contributing to its cultural diversity and economic growth.
