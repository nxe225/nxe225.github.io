Section 1 - Economy

Agriculture

Agriculture is a leading force of the economy and allowed Belize to have a strong post-pandemic rebound. Innovative and ecological bond restructuring significantly lowered public debt and expanded marine protections.

Tourism

Tourism has become the mainstay of the economy. Current concerns include the countrys heavy foreign debt burden, high crime rates, high unemployment combined with a majority youth population, growing involvement in the Mexican and South American drug trade, and one of the highest HIV/AIDS prevalence rates in Central America.

GPD Growth

The real GPD growth rate increased 17.1% in 2021, 8.73% in 2022, and 4.54% in 2023

Section 2 - Culture

Languages

Over 60% of the population in Belize is speaking english, which is the countries native language.

Ethnic Groups

Belize is made up of 52.9% Mestizo, 25.9% Creole, 11.3%, 6.1% Garifuna, 3.9% East Indian, 3.6% Mennonite, 1.2%, Asian 1%, 1.2% white, and 0.3% is unknown (2010 est.).

Migration

Migration continues to transform Belize's population. About 16% of Belizeans live abroad, while immigrants constitute approximately 15% of Belize's population.
